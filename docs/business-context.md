# Business Context

Insurance organisations rely heavily on structured, reliable, and well‑governed data to operate effectively. Core business processes such as customer onboarding, policy management, claims processing, payments, and assessments all generate data that must be captured consistently and stored in a way that supports both operational workflows and analytical needs.

A clear and well‑designed data model enables:

- Accurate and timely reporting  
- Faster and more consistent claims processing  
- Improved risk assessment and pricing  
- Better customer experience  
- Reduced data duplication and inconsistencies  
- Stronger regulatory and compliance alignment  

This project models a simplified version of an insurance claims environment. While the domain is broad and complex in real organisations, the goal here is to demonstrate the foundational modelling skills required for a Junior Data Modeller role: understanding business processes, identifying key entities, defining relationships, and translating requirements into structured data artefacts.

The model focuses on five core business objects:

- **Customer** – the individual or entity holding insurance policies  
- **Policy** – the insurance product purchased by the customer  
- **Claim** – a request for compensation under a policy  
- **Payment** – disbursements made to settle a claim  
- **Assessment** – evaluations performed to determine claim validity and value  

These objects represent the backbone of most insurance data ecosystems and form the basis for reporting, analytics, and operational decision‑making.
